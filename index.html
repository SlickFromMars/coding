TSA Coding II

What is Coding?

In coding, you will be tested on your knowledge of various coding languages through an exam. You can study and prepare for this all year in order to score well. If you score higher than enough teams, you will make it to semifinals. When your team of 2 makes it to the semifinals, you will participate in a coding challenge. This usually consists of 5 prompts that you need to follow, and program, in the language of your choice. Anyone can join coding, but we have a limit of 2 people.

How Can I Learn Coding?

There are many great resources to learn some coding! Khan Academy offers a course on some basic and advanced programming with HTML, Javascript, and a few other languages. Another amazing resource is w3schools, which has exercises and information for many different coding languages. CodeChef also offers information on many different languages. Skills You Will Use

This event provides opportunity for students to build and develop leadership and 21st century skills such as: Communication, Collaboration/Social Skills, Initiative, Problem Solving/Risk Taking, Critical Thinking, Perseverance/Grit, Creativity, Relationship Building/Teamwork, Dependability/Integrity, and Flexibility/Adaptability.

Career Connections and Other Competitions

There are many careers in the coding field, such as, computer software engineer, mathematician, website developer and video game programmer. If you are interested in this, here are some other TSA competitions you might be interested in: Software Development, System Control Technology, Video Game Design, Virtual Reality Visualization, Webmaster.
